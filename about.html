Prickly Pair Caravan

About me

What is Prickly Pair Caravan?

It is a way for you to try new board games at a low cost. I do weekly themed board game nights at Quarter Celtic. Tuesday nights we are at the Juan Tabo location and Wednesday nights at the San Mateo location. Both 6-8pm

Are the game nights really free?

Yes! There is no cost to enter but any purchase you make at Quarter Celtic is greatly appreciated! They have helped me so much to make this a reality so even a single beer purchase is a great way to support these nights. And no worries at all if you spill a drink or get food on a game. That's the cost of doing business babeyyy there is no charge for damaging a game.

Where is the rest of your website?

I am doing an overhaul on my inventory page and recommendations page. I have hidden them for now until i have time to finish that work.

What inspired you to make the cart?

Imitation is the highest form of flattery. I saw a post of someone else who did something similar. They converted a pedal ice cream cart to sell board games out of. That combined with my love of board games gave me the motivation to make Prickly Pair Caravan a reality!

How did you get into board games?

A coworker invited me to their board game night in the winter of 2023. I had only ever played mainstream board games before and had no idea there was anything beyond that. From there the seed was planted and I hope Prickly Pair Caravan brings you some of that joy as well!

How do you get your prices so low?

A mixture of sales, discount codes, and meeting free shipping requirements allows me to sell games at or below $15. You can find similar prices online, maybe even a couple dollars less but shipping adds on 7 dollars minumum to your order.

What are your margins?

I believe the most profit I make off a game is 20% with many games being around 5%-10% after accounting for tax. Combined with how much time I spend looking for games really makes this a labor of love. I'm not quitting my day job anytime soon ha.

How did you build the cart?

I purchased these design plans for a cotton candy cart and then made modifications of my own. The biggest being wheelbarrow tires instead of bicycle due to the amount of goatheads here.

Plans for the future?

Currently we are only at Quarter Celtic. I would like to expand into artisan markets, I have something in the works for that! I can't sell my normal inventory but by mid October 2024 I hope to have a couple prototypes ready.
